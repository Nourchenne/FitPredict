TITLE: Complete Guide to Obesity

DEFINITION
Obesity is a chronic medical condition characterized by excessive body fat accumulation that increases the risk of health problems.

BMI CLASSIFICATION
BMI = weight (kg) / height (m²)

- Underweight: < 18.5
- Normal weight: 18.5 – 24.9
- Overweight: 25 – 29.9
- Obesity Class I: 30 – 34.9
- Obesity Class II: 35 – 39.9
- Obesity Class III (Severe): ≥ 40

CAUSES OF OBESITY
1. Excess calorie intake
2. Sedentary lifestyle
3. Genetic predisposition
4. Hormonal disorders
5. Emotional eating
6. Sleep deprivation
7. Ultra-processed food consumption

HEALTH RISKS
- Type 2 Diabetes
- Hypertension
- Cardiovascular diseases
- Stroke
- Sleep apnea
- Fatty liver disease
- Joint problems
- Certain cancers

SYMPTOMS
- Excess body fat
- Shortness of breath
- Fatigue
- Sweating
- Snoring
- Reduced mobility

TREATMENT APPROACH
1. Calorie deficit diet
2. Regular physical activity
3. Behavioral therapy
4. Medical supervision
5. Medication (in some cases)
6. Bariatric surgery (severe cases)

PREVENTION STRATEGIES
- Balanced diet
- Regular exercise
- Portion control
- Reduce sugar intake
- Avoid sugary drinks
- Sleep 7–9 hours
- Manage stress
