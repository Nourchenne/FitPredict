TITLE: Practical Meal Planning for Obesity Management

GOAL
Build a weekly eating structure that supports fat loss while preserving energy and adherence.

CORE RULES
1. Use a moderate calorie deficit (typically 300-500 kcal/day).
2. Include protein in each main meal.
3. Fill half the plate with vegetables.
4. Prefer whole-food carbs over refined sugar.
5. Keep hydration consistent (about 2-3L/day unless medically restricted).

SIMPLE PLATE METHOD
- 1/2 plate: vegetables (raw/cooked)
- 1/4 plate: lean protein (fish, chicken, eggs, tofu, legumes)
- 1/4 plate: quality carbs (oats, potatoes, quinoa, brown rice)
- Add healthy fat in small amounts (olive oil, nuts, seeds)

SNACK STRATEGY
- Choose protein + fiber: yogurt + fruit, apple + peanut butter, hummus + carrots.
- Avoid liquid calories and frequent sugary snacks.

PORTION CONTROL TIPS
- Eat slowly and stop at comfortable fullness.
- Use smaller plates and pre-portion snacks.
- Avoid eating directly from packages.

WEEKLY PREP CHECKLIST
- Batch-cook 2 proteins.
- Prepare 1 grain base and chopped vegetables.
- Plan 2 quick breakfasts and 2 quick dinners.
- Keep healthy snacks ready.

COMMON MISTAKES
- Extreme restriction and rebound eating.
- Skipping protein.
- Underestimating sauces/oils.
- Inconsistent meal timing followed by overeating.
