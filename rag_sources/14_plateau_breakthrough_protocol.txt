TITLE: Plateau Breakthrough Protocol (When Progress Stalls)

DEFINITION
A plateau means measurable progress is flat despite following the plan for at least 2-3 weeks.

STEP-BY-STEP
1. Validate data quality
- Track body weight 3-7 times/week and use weekly average.
- Check waist circumference and photos.
- Confirm meal tracking accuracy (oils, sauces, snacks, drinks).

2. Check adherence first
- Aim for at least 80-90% adherence to plan.
- If adherence is low, fix consistency before changing calories.

3. Increase activity slightly
- Add 1,500-3,000 steps/day or one extra cardio session.
- Keep changes small and sustainable.

4. Adjust calories modestly
- Reduce daily intake by 100-200 kcal if adherence is already good.
- Keep protein high to preserve muscle.

5. Manage recovery
- Sleep 7-9h.
- Reduce stress and avoid all-or-nothing behavior.

6. Reassess after 2 weeks
- Keep one variable change at a time.

DO NOT
- Slash calories aggressively.
- Add too much cardio suddenly.
- Change everything at once.
